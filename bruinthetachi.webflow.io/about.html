Beyond the college years, our close-knit brotherhood endures, offering a helping hand in times of triumph or challenge. We stand united, providing unwavering support and fostering a lasting camaraderie that leaves a legacy of lifelong connections among our brothers - a true testament to the power of genuine brotherhood and the lifelong impact it has on each member's life.

Our Mission
‍
At Theta Chi, our mission is simple yet profound: to develop resolute, well-rounded leaders who embody our core values of Truth, Temperance, and Tolerance. We strive to create an inclusive and supportive environment that empowers our members to thrive both personally and academically. Our fraternity fosters an unwavering sense of brotherhood, instilling values that resonate long after college life, guiding our members to lead with integrity and compassion in all aspects of their lives.

Our Vision
‍
Theta Chi fraternity's vision revolves around diversity and inclusion. We believe in creating a welcoming and supportive environment that celebrates the unique backgrounds and perspectives of our members. Embracing diversity enriches our brotherhood, fostering cultural exchange, understanding, and mutual respect, while our commitment to inclusion ensures that every member feels valued and empowered. Together, we stand united, stronger as a diverse and inclusive fraternity, making a positive impact in our community and the world beyond.

Our Principles
‍
Theta Chi fraternity is guided by three fundamental principles: Truth, Temperance, and Tolerance. Truth promotes transparent communication and accountability, fostering a culture of trust. Temperance encourages a balanced and disciplined lifestyle, instilling responsibility. Tolerance embraces diversity, creating an inclusive environment where mutual respect and unity thrive. These principles shape well-rounded individuals, embodying brotherhood, leadership, and service, leaving a positive impact on our fraternity.

Our Founders
‍
Theta Chi fraternity was founded with a vision that continues to shape our identity and values. Established on April 10, 1856, by two remarkable young men, Frederick Norton Freeman and Arthur Chase, at Norwich University in Vermont, their vision was to create a fraternity that transcended the traditional norms of social organizations. Today, their vision lives on, guiding us to cultivate leaders of character who uphold these timeless values, creating a lasting impact on our fraternity and the world we serve. As we honor the legacy of our founders, we remain dedicated to fulfilling their vision, standing united as a brotherhood that exemplifies the essence of Theta Chi.
